About the Artist

1950's and 60's futurism and vintage toy design serves as the main inspiration of my 3D design work. Vintage toy package design and promotional posters serve as the driving force behind my aesthetic, as they inspire a sense of childhood whimsy, and inspire an intergenerational idea that there is always more to explore, discover and create. This mid-1900's aesthetic depicts the inevitability of science, cleverly illustrated in the fantastical imagery of flying cars and colorful accents. It is my goal to reflect this in my toy design work. My medium of choice is Computer Aided Design and manufacturing techniques, utilizing the strength of plastic and metal as a way of expressing form and function. I marry these modern, new age ways of designing with traditional painting and dying techniques to bring my pieces new life. This process is reflected in my package designs. The process starts in Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop, then the finer details are added though the use of colored pencil. I utilize both the endless possibility of digital design and the fulfilling process of traditional methodology in all of my work.

Through my work, it is my intention that the viewer will be able to step outside of themselves and get lost in the world of another person. I am striving to bring the tradition of toy design into the twenty-first century, without losing the imagination of the previous generations. I urge the viewers of my work to recall a time when anything seemed possible and when “science” was synonymous with “magic”.
